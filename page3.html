Resident Assistant Job Description

GENERAL STATEMENT OF DUTIES:
Resident Assistants (RAs) are student staff members living in the residence halls who facilitate opportunities that have a positive impact on students living in the halls. RAs build connections with and amongst individuals living in their communities, through one on one interactions and community programming and meetings. RAs foster inclusive communities and assist in maintaining a safe and positive environment for all students living within the residence halls. RAs are held to a high standard of accountability and personal conduct due to the unique role-modeling needed in this position. As such, RAs are an extension of the Residential Center and act as staff members in the office. RAs should conduct themselves in a manner that is consistent with mission of the Residential Center and Bentley University.
 
SUPERVISION:
RAs report through the Residential Center Staff Structure. RAs report specifically to a Residence Director within a building(s). RAs receive ongoing feedback on their performance throughout their contract period. RAs receive an official evaluation during and at the end of each contract period. RAs will receive information on our performance management process during RA training. Additionally, the Administrator on Duty serves as a supervisor to RAs during their on-duty rotation.
 
POSITION RESPONSIBILITIES:
Below is a brief synopsis of general position responsibilities required by each RA. RAs will be required to follow all responsibilities and duties outlined in detail in the RA manual. 
Community Building and Intentional Interactions: (35%)
Facilitate student learning through strategies including one on one interactions with residents in the community, completion of bulletin boards, floor/community meetings, events/programs, and (if applicable) Defined Community initiatives.
Get to know and make every effort to interact with every resident in your community and report that information to your supervisor.
Demonstrate a commitment to role modeling a responsible and inclusive community environment. 
Utilize “The Housing Director” log notes to document student interactions and communicate these updates to your supervisor.
Incident Response: (25%)
Abide by and enforce university and residence hall policies. Document violations of policy and report through the on-duty structure as outlined in the RA manual. 
Serve as an RA on duty in the residence hall(s) assigned. Each building is staffed by an RA(s) from 7:30pm until 8:30am the following morning. RAs will then facilitate an on-call duty during the weekend hours outside the staffed hours. RAs will hold onto the official RA duty cell phone during this time.
RAs on duty are required to be visible in their assigned residence hall community from 7:30pm to Midnight (Sunday through Thursday) and 7:30pm to 2:00am (Friday and Saturday).
Staff and Training Responsibilities: (20%)
RAs will complete a training period at the start of each semester. Those dates are outline each year by the department and will be communicated during the RA selection process. Staff are expected to take part in all aspects of training. 
Participate in Residential Center search processes as assigned to recruit and interview new staff (student and professional).
Attend and actively participate in staff meetings each week. Staff meetings will generally be two hours on Tuesdays sometime between 7:00pm and 11:00pm. 
Meet on a bi-weekly basis (or more regularly as needed) with your supervisor
Be a positive, active, and engaged member of the staff team and the overall Residential Center Department team;
Housing Services Responsibilities: (20%)
Actively participate all hall openings and closings (first year and upper-class).
Ensure the completion of Room Condition Reports for each residential bed space and common space within the residential community.
Submit work orders and communicate facility issues and needs to your supervisor in a timely manner.


CONTRACT PERIOD:
The RA position is a full academic year (consecutive Fall and Spring semesters) commitment. The contract period includes all mandatory trainings prior to the start of each semester and closing responsibilities at the end of each semester (the day after closing). The contract period also require RAs to fulfill duty responsibilities during scheduled breaks/holidays observed by the University; including Fall Break, Thanksgiving Break, and Spring Break. When the University is closed (winter break/summer break), RAs are not required to stay on campus, but RAs do have the option to remain on campus in the Winter/Summer RA position, if the department deems necessary.   

COMPENSATION:
The Residential Center covers the full cost of a resident assistants housing assignment on campus. Due to the assigned style of living, RAs living in the Halls and Suites, will have meal plan covered as a part of their assignment. RAs living in the Apartments will not be compensated with a meal plan through the Residential Center, as each apartment is equipped with essential cooking units. All compensation will be credited to accounts at the beginning of each semester.
 
REQUIREMENTS:
RAs must be undergraduate students enrolled in a degree granting program. Current RAs can petition to return to the position during their graduate year. More information can be obtained about this opportunity through their Residence Director.  
A minimum cumulative GPA of 2.5 is required at time of hire and two weeks prior to the commencement of duties in August. RAs must maintain a minimum cumulative GPA of 2.5 to remain in the position. Each semester, the Residential Center will review all RA cumulative grade point averages. If an RA’s GPA does not meet these requirements, the RA is no longer eligible and their contract will be reevaluated. 
Resident Assistants must be free of current student conduct/Title IX sanctions and have resolved any pending conduct charges two weeks prior to the commencement of RA duties in August or one week prior to the commencement of duties when starting the position at any other time during the year. Each semester, the Residential Center will review all Resident Assistant conduct histories. 
If a staff member is found responsible for a University or residence hall policy violation after the time of hire, a conversation regarding employment will be discussed with the result ranging from a verbal warning to termination.
Extracurricular activities and other jobs will be limited to 20 hours a week. Exceeding the 20 hour a week limit would require permission from your supervisor.
Newly hired RAs for the Fall 2019 will be required to complete a 5 week workshop series dedicated to further development within the RA role. This workshop series will take place bi-weekly beginning on Wednesday, August 29th and will run until October 24th. Workshops will take place during the activity period beginning at 2pm. This is a requirement of the RA position. 


ADDITIONAL INFORMATION:
CONFIDENTIALITY:
The Residential Center strives to respect the rights of residents as it relates to their personal and professional life. Information about a resident will only be shared by you to the department. It is important to assure a resident that you will not share any private information with other residents, RAs, friends, or faculty. Confidential also encompasses any information that you receive or submit through the Housing Director and Advocate software systems.
 
CRISIS RESPONSE:
Title IX and Cleary Act Campus Security Authority– RAs are responsible employees at the University. RAs have the duty to report incidents of sexual, romantic violence or other crimes. If a student tells a responsible employee about an incident of sexual, romantic violence or crimes, the student has the right to expect the University to take immediate and appropriate steps to investigate what happened and to resolve the matter promptly and equitably. A responsible employee must report all relevant details about the alleged sexual violence shared by the student, including the names of all students involved; the nature of the alleged incident; and the date, time, and specific location of the alleged incident.
 
Student Crisis Response – RAs are expected to report student crisis incidents through the Residential Center reporting structure immediately through their supervisor or the Administrator on Duty. As outlined in the RA manual, an RA should contact their supervisor during normal business hours, and the AOD should be utilized whenever the Residential Center is closed.
 
Emergency Response– RAs will be trained in all aspects of crisis response. At times, the Residential Center may call upon the entire department to assist in emergency situations (inclement weather, tragedy, etc.) RAs understand that they may be required to work additional hours and fulfill additional responsibilities outside of their day-to-day work load. The Residential Center will never compromise the safety of student staff when it comes to emergency response. RAs are never to put themselves in harm’s way and should communicate that with the department if they feel they are.
